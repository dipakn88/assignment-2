Geography of Nepal

Nepal is a landlocked country located between China and India. Known for its diverse geography, Nepal is home to some of the world's tallest mountains, including Mount Everest, the highest point on Earth. The country ranges from the humid Terai plains in the south to the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas in the north.

Cultural Heritage

Nepal is a melting pot of cultures, languages, and traditions. With over 100 ethnic groups, the country boasts a rich diversity of festivals, music, and dances. The Nepali calendar is filled with celebrations, from Dashain to Tihar, each representing the vibrant spiritual life of the Nepalese people.

Tourism in Nepal

As the gateway to the Himalayas, Nepal attracts thousands of tourists every year. Adventurers flock to the country for trekking, mountaineering, and exploring its pristine landscapes. The Annapurna Circuit and the Everest Base Camp trek are among the most famous routes in the world, offering unmatched natural beauty.

Did You Know?

Mount Everest, known as Sagarmatha in Nepali, stands at 8,848 meters (29,029 feet) and continues to grow by a few millimeters each year due to tectonic activity.
